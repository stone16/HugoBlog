## Design Context

### Users
Tech-curious readers — developers, founders, and people interested in AI and building products. They arrive from Twitter (@stometaverse) or search, scan for quality signals, and either read or bounce within seconds. They read on mobile and desktop, often in dark environments. International audience (Chinese + English content).

### Brand Personality
**Professional, Thoughtful, Bold** — A founder sharing deep insights with a confident voice. Not academic, not casual. The tone of someone who's built real systems at Amazon/Flexport and is now building their own company (ZentaStone).

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Premium editorial publication. Think Stripe's blog — polished, confident, with purposeful use of color. Not a developer blog, not a SaaS landing page.
- **References**: Stripe's blog/press (premium polish, gradient use with restraint, strong typography)
- **Anti-references**: SaaS landing pages (no hero metrics, feature grids, pricing tables). This is a publication, not a product.
- **Theme**: Dark mode only. The current purple (#a855f7) / cyan (#06b6d4) gradient palette.
- **Typography**: Plus Jakarta Sans (display), Inter (body), JetBrains Mono (code)

### Design Principles
1. **Content is king** — Typography, spacing, and readability matter more than decoration. Every visual element serves the content.
2. **Earned attention** — Visual accents (gradients, glows, color) are used sparingly to highlight what matters, not sprinkled everywhere.
3. **Professional credibility** — The design should make a reader think "this person is serious" within 5 seconds.
4. **Editorial restraint** — A publication aesthetic: generous whitespace, clear hierarchy, minimal chrome.
5. **Intentional, not impressive** — Every design choice should feel purposeful. No decoration for decoration's sake.
